Lifestyle

Drew Allison Carey, also known as Drew Carey, was born on May 23, 1958, and grew up in Cleveland, Ohio, with his two elder brothers.

Sadly, when he was eight years old, his father was laid to rest due to a heart attack.

Further, in 1975, he attended and graduated from James Ford Rhodes High School.

Since Carey was a youngster, the famous actor has been interested in music, and he used to play the trumpet in the marching band in high school.

Furthermore, he continued his study at Kent State University after graduating from high school.

Unfortunately, Carey was dismissed from university twice for dreadful academic performance before quitting college just after three years.

In 1980, he enrolled in the United States Marine Corps Reserve after dropping out of college.

Furthermore, Drew Carey served as a Marine for six years as a field radio operator in Ohio.

In fact, in 1986, he topped an open mic competition and was elected Master of Ceremonies at the Cleveland Comedy Club.

Consequently, He spent the following five years presenting at comedy gigs in Cleveland and Los Angeles.

In addition, he spent several months in Las Vegas in 1983, working as a supermarket cashier and a server at Denny’s.

Drew Carey: Career

Stand-up career

In 1985, Drew Carey commenced his comedic career by taking David Lawrence’s advice and going to the libraries to acquire materials on how to make gags.

Further, he was named Master of Ceremonies at the Cleveland Comedy Club in 1998.

When he participated in the 1988 Star Search, he first gained national attention as a stand-up comedian.

Carey was indeed a stand-up comic at the time of his appearance on The Tonight Show, Starring Johnny Carson in November 1991.

Likewise, he appeared on HBO’s 14th Annual Young Comedians Special and for the first time on a famous talk show, Late Night with David Letterman.

Drew Carey: Human Cartoon, his own stand-up comedy special, aired on Showtime networks in 1994.

As a result, he won a Cable ACE Award for Best Writing in the same year.
