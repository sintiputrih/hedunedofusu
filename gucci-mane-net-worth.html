Gucci Mane Biography and Career

Radric Delantic Davis was born in Bessemer, Alabama, on February 12, 1980. His father was on the run from authorities for distributing crack cocaine and heroin when he was born in Detroit, Michigan.

While his mother was at college, Davis was reared by his paternal grandmother. Mane was introduced to rap and hip hop at a young age by his elder half-brother Victor, who took him to a concert including Run-DMC, the Beastie Boys, and LL Cool J. Mane and his mother moved to Atlanta in 1989.

He grew up in a high-crime region. Mane started selling drugs in junior high. In seventh grade, he spent his Christmas money on crack cocaine to sell to others. Mane was a good student and well-liked by his peers in high school. Despite having a side career as a drug dealer, Mane did not use narcotics during his first few years in the business.

Mane had been dealing drugs for two years when he was stopped on his bicycle, held a gun at his head, and robbed. He began carrying a weapon after that. Mane earned a 3.0 GPA and a scholarship at Georgia Perimeter College after graduating from high school in 1998.

He was dismissed from college in 2001 after an undercover police officer discovered him in possession of crack cocaine. He received a 90-day county jail sentence as well as probation. Gucci was a drug dealer before becoming a rapper, and he was arrested for cocaine possession in 2001.

He began to take music more seriously after being released from county jail, and hoped to make a career out of it. Gucci self-released his first mixtape, ‘La Flare,’ and then decided to start his own record company, which is how La Flare Entertainment was founded.

Gucci Mane Personal Life

On October 17, 2017, Gucci Mane married Keyshia Ka’oir. The wedding was paid for by BET, who built a 10-episode TV series dubbed “The Mane Event” it, with Ka’oir orchestrating the event and walking down the aisle on a diamond-studded carpet.

After performing at an Atlanta Hawks basketball game, Mane proposed to Ka’oir. The couple has a son named Ice Davis and a daughter named Iceland Ka’oir Davis. He disclosed in his memoirs that he had a son born in 2007 whom he was unaware of until 2016.
