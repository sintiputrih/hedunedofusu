Early Life

Lang Lang is the son of Lang Guoren and Zhou Xiulan, born on 14 June 1982 in Shenyang, Liaoning, China. Lang’s father is also a music lover, as he used to play a two-stringed bowed musical instrument called erhu. At the age of two, he got inspired by the cartoon series Tom and Jerry to play the piano. He started learning piano at the minor age of three from Professor Zhu Ya-Fen. He had won the Shenyang Piano Competition when he was just five years old.

Later, Lang was sent to the conservatory, where Professor Zhao Ping-Guo trained him. Due to his Professor’s guidance and Lang’s hard work, he won the Xing Hai Piano Competition Cup, which was held in Beijing. During fourth International Competition, Lang won the award for his outstanding performance in 1994. At the age of 13, Lang got the most significant opportunity to play the Op. 10 and Op.

Twenty-five compositions composed by Frédéric Chopin at the Beijing Concert Hall. During the International Tchaikovsky Competition for Young Musicians by Japan, Lang was placed at no.1 position. At a very young age, he has accomplished many achievements in his career. He also appeared at the China National Symphony’s opening concert, which was attended by the president of China Jiang Zemin.

For better opportunities, Lang moved to America with his father in 1997. There he attended the Curtis Institute of Music in Pennsylvania. That was the turning point in his career.
