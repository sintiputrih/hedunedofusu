Laura Rutledge Biography

Laura Rutledge (née McKeeman;) is an ESPN and SEC Network reporter and host. She is also a Miss Florida 2012 winner and an American beauty pageant winner from St. Petersburg, Florida.

Laura Rutledge Age

She was born on 2 October 1988 in , St. Petersburg, Florida, United States.

Laura Rutledge Height

She stands at a height of 5 feet and 10 inches tall.

Laura Rutledge Education

Rutledge earned a bachelor’s degree in broadcast journalism from the University of Florida, where she also received the Red Barber Award for Excellence in Broadcasting in 2011. Rutledge is a Miracle League Ambassador, an organization that constructs baseball fields for disabled youngsters.

Laura Rutledge Parents

Rutledge is the loving daughter of Robert McKeeman (father) and Deborah McKeeman (mother).  Her mother, Deborah is her best friend and the greatest adventure partner.

Laura Rutledge Husband

She is happily married to professional baseball player Josh Rutledge. The duo tied the knot in 2013. They reside in Birmingham, Alabama.

Laura Rutledge ESPN

Rutledge is the host of SEC Network’s SEC Nation and ESPN’s NFL Live, which airs every weekday throughout the year. Rutledge has gradually grown her portfolio since joining the organization in 2014 as a reporter for SEC Network and ESPN. In June 2020, she was chosen host of NFL Live, and also presents SEC Nation.  In 2021, she was in her fifth year as host and sixth year overall with the show. Rutledge was a contributor on Get Up before joining NFL Live.

Additionally, she’s also hosted red carpet coverage at the Home Depot College Football Awards, the ESPYs, and the NFL Draft, as well as anchoring SportsCenter. Rutledge has also contributed to live-event programming for college football, basketball, gymnastics, baseball, and softball on ESPN and SEC Network. Moreover, she was a regular on ESPN’s coverage of the College Football Playoff, NCAA Women’s Gymnastics Championships, and both the Women’s College World Series and the College World Series. Rutledge was a sports anchor for CNN’s New Day and Early Start before joining ESPN. She also worked alongside Robin Meade on HLN’s Morning Express and for Fox Sports.
