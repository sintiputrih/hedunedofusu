Malik Willis career

Willis participated in 15 games while he was at Auburn between 2017 and 2018. In those two years, he ran for 309 yards and two scores in addition to completing 11 of 14 throws for 69 yards and a score.

Following a quarterback duel with Bo Nix in 2019, Willis transferred to Liberty University and sat out the 2019 season due to transfer regulations.

He was appointed the team’s starting quarterback in 2020. He passed for six touchdowns and ran for one against Southern Mississippi.

Willis received the 2021 Bobby Bowden Award, given to the finest college football player who best exemplifies being a Christian student-athlete, as well as the 2020 Dudley Award, given to the greatest college football player in the state of Virginia. In both the 2020 Cure Bowl and the 2021 LendingTree Bowl, he was awarded MVP.

The Tennessee Titans selected Willis in the third round of the 2022 NFL Draft (86th overall). He was viewed as a surprising selection in the draft because some had him going as high as second overall. On July 23, 2022, he agreed to a four-year rookie contract.

After the Titans benched Ryan Tannehill in the third quarter of Week 2’s game against the Buffalo Bills, Willis made his NFL debut. In the 41-7 defeat, he threw for six yards on one of his four tries, completed one pass, and gained 16 yards on the ground.

When Ryan Tannehill became ill in Week 8, Willis was appointed the starter against the Texans. Willis won the game 17-10 in his debut start, completing 6 of 10 passes for 55 yards and one interception.

Week 9’s game against the Chiefs saw Willis start in place of an ailing Ryan Tannehill for a second straight week. In the 20-17 overtime defeat, he completed 5 of his 16 pass attempts for 80 yards.

Tannehill, who had worsened an ankle injury, was replaced by Willis in the starting lineup for Week 16’s game against the Houston Texans. He completed 14 of his 23 throws during the game, totaling 99 passing yards and two interceptions as the Titans fell short, 19–14.
