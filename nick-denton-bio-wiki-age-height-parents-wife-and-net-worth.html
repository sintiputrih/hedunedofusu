Nick Denton Career

Denton began his career as a journalist with the Financial Times, a British daily business newspaper. He co-wrote a book about the collapse of Barings Bank, the oldest merchant bank in London, called All That Glitters (1996).

He was a co-founder in 1998 of a social networking site, First Tuesday. That same year Denton co-founded media-monitoring service Moreover Technologies with David Galbraith and Angus Bankes, schoolmates from UCS. Shortly after, he moved to San Francisco in the United States, and in the summer of 2002, he moved to New York City.

Denton founded Gawker Media in 2002, initially running the company out of his apartment in New York City’s SoHo. Gawker Media initially had only two internet sites — Gawker (a news and gossip site) and Gizmodo (a gadgets and technology blog).

Gawker Media blogs expanded to include Jezebel (aimed at women), and Deadspin (a sports site), as well as Lifehacker (tips to live life better), Jalopnik (automotive culture), io9 (science, futurism, and science fiction), and Kotaku (video games and culture).

Nick Denton Awards Accomplishments and Achievements

  • Founded Gawker Media in 2002, which quickly became a leading source of online news and gossip.
  • Helped pioneer the practice of “blogging” and created a new model for online journalism that emphasized transparency and immediacy.
  • Built a network of websites that attracted millions of readers and earned praise for their bold, irreverent reporting style.
  • Received several awards for his work in journalism, including the George Polk Award for his coverage of the Bill O’Reilly sexual harassment scandal.
  • Sold Gawker Media to Univision in 2016 for $135 million, after the company was forced into bankruptcy due to a lawsuit funded by billionaire Peter Thiel.
  • Continues to be involved in media and technology through his investments in various startups and his work as a consultant and advisor.
  • Advocated for free speech and press freedom throughout his career, even in the face of legal and financial challenges.
