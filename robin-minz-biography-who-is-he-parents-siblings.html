Robin Minz Biography: Who Is He? Parents And Siblings

Publish date: 2024-09-06

Robin Minz biography reveals the details of a lesser-known cricketer who became the first tribal cricketer selected for the Indian Premier League despite his humble family background.

Robin Minz, a hard-hitting wicketkeeper-batsman, created history in 2022, becoming the first tribal player to get picked in the world’s most prestigious T20 league, the IPL.

Representing Jharkhand in domestic cricket, his belligerent left-handed batting and neat glovework first caught the eye during the IPL auctions.

His aggressive stroke play, especially against pacers, led to an intense bidding war between IPL heavyweights, the Mumbai Indians, and new entrants, the Gujarat Titans.

Minz’s selection represents a landmark moment for talented tribal cricketers in India eyeing the IPL.

Robin Minz Biography

Although Robin Minz hasn’t made his professional cricket debut yet, his skills have attracted attention from various IPL franchises and cricket circuits.

At 8, he ventured into cricket and later transitioned fully into the sport after completing his 10th grade.

Despite not having played first-class cricket, he captained Jharkhand’s U-19 and U-25 teams, showcasing his leadership potential and talent.

The 21-year-old’s remarkable talent led to his selection by the Mumbai Indians for a training camp and even earned him a spot in the England training program to further hone his skills.

His batting prowess was evident when he scored an unbeaten 73 off 35 deliveries in an invitational T20 tournament in Odisha.

During the IPL auctions, the wicketkeeper-batsman’s base price of INR 20 lakh escalated dramatically.

The Mumbai Indians and Gujarat Titans engaged in a bidding war, ultimately landing him a staggering INR 3.6 crore deal, 18 times his base price.

Although unsold in the 2023 auction, Minz’s potential was recognized by multiple IPL teams, including the Lucknow Supergiants, Delhi Capitals, and Kolkata Knight Riders.

Minz looks up to the iconic cricketer MS Dhoni as his idol, particularly admiring Dhoni’s wicketkeeping skills, quick thinking, and composed demeanor.

His passion for the sport and dedication to improving his craft have been evident throughout his journey.

The young athlete’s rise in the cricketing world, his historic IPL deal, and his determination to excel in the sport inspire, especially, budding cricketers from tribal backgrounds.

His journey from a small town in Jharkhand to the grand stage of the IPL exemplifies grit, talent, and the impact of familial support on an athlete’s success.

Robin Minz Parents

Robin Minz’s father, Mr. Binz Minz, was an army officer who retired after many years of service.

Despite his army background, Mr. Binz recognized his young son’s natural cricketing talent early on.

After retirement, Mr. Binz became a security guard at the Birsa Munda Airport in Jharkhand to support his family.

Though the job was modest, it encouraged him to develop his cricketing skills further.

Mr. Binz spent evenings bowling with his son, training him on shot selection, teaching game tactics, and motivating him to excel as a batsman.

He even got the wicketkeeper-batsman to participate in local cricket tournaments. Mr. Binz’s background as an army man likely instilled values of discipline and hard work in young Robin, too.

The athlete credits his father for recognizing his talent, pushing him to work hard to hone it, and being the driving force propelling him to become a professional cricketer.

Meanwhile, the cricketer’s mother was a homemaker who took care of household responsibilities, allowing the father-son duo to focus on cricket practice.

She likely supported him emotionally through the ups and downs of competitive cricket.

Robin Minz Siblings

Robin Minz grew up with an older sister and a younger brother. His sister helped look after him while their parents were working.

She accompanied him early on during cricket coaching sessions. His younger brother looked up to him once he started getting selected for local cricket teams and tournaments.

The success of his elder sibling motivated the cricketer’s brother to pick up cricket.

The brothers likely practiced together under their father’s guidance, which provided camaraderie.

It also taught healthy competition between them, spurring them to perform better. He may have helped mentor his younger brother in cricket as well.

Therefore, the athlete’s siblings were supplementary to his cricketing success story, providing emotional support, competition, and additional motivation.

So while Minz’s parents, especially his father, Mr. Binz Minz, played the most pivotal role in shaping his cricketing journey, his siblings were a positive influence, too, during his formative years.

Despite lacking a privileged background, the love and support of his family enabled the wicketkeeper-batsman to recognize and relentlessly pursue his cricketing dreams.
