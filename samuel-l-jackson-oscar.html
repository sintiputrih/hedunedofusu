What Did Samuel L. Jackson Win an Oscar For? Inside His Historic Win

Publish date: 2024-07-30

Samuel L. Jackson is a legendary and prolific actor who has left his imprint on the entertainment business. 

Jackson has become one of the most recognizable and renowned performers of his generation due to his imposing presence, unique voice, and explosive performances. Throughout his long career, he has played various roles — from villains to heroes — and has always provided remarkable performances that have captivated audiences worldwide.

He has played some iconic roles, and people are often curious about how many accolades Jackson has won to date for movies and shows he has worked on.

What did Samuel L. Jackson win an Oscar for?

Jackson has never won an Oscar for a movie. He was nominated for Pulp Fiction in 1995 for the category of Best Supporting Actor. 

However, he lost the Oscars to Martin Landau, who won the award for Ed Wood. Since then, Jackson was never nominated for the Oscars despite the excellent movies he was working on.

Samuel L. Jackson won Academy Honorary Award in 2022. (Source: Twitter)

However, in 2022, he was honored with the Academy Honorary Award. His long-time friend Denzel Washington presented him with an honorary Academy Award for lifetime achievement at the Governors Awards on Friday in a non-televised event.

Upon winning the award, Jackson said, “So I’m really, really proud to receive this statue. Who knows, that little kid from Chattanooga, Tennessee, watching movies, wishing he could be here to get the votes of people who run an organization like this that says, ‘Hey, I deserve something like this,’ thank you, so much.”

Samuel L. Jackson’s bio and movies

Jackson was born on December 21, 1948, in Washington, D.C., to a working-class family and initially pursued a degree in marine biology. However, his love of performing drove him to change paths to pursue theatre at Morehouse College in Atlanta. 

Jackson’s passion and talent immediately became apparent, and he began to establish himself as a stage actor, appearing in both on and off-Broadway shows.

He made his film debut in the comedy Together for Days in the 1980s, transitioning from stage to cinema. Despite early disappointments and hardships, he persevered and gradually achieved notoriety for his performances in films such as Spike Lee’s School Daze and Do the Right Thing. 

These collaborations marked the start of successful cooperation between the two, with Jackson later appearing in numerous more of Lee’s films, including Jungle Fever and Malcolm X.

However, it was his performance as Jules Winnfield in Quentin Tarantino’s cult masterpiece Pulp Fiction that catapulted Jackson to international notoriety. His performance as the Bible-quoting hitman won him a nomination for Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor.

The picture’s success opened doors for Jackson, and he went on to work with some of the industry’s most famous directors, including Steven Spielberg, Martin Scorsese, and M. Night Shyamalan.

Jackson worked with filmmaker Shyamalan in the supernatural thriller Unbreakable, in which he portrayed Elijah Price, also known as Mr. Glass. He reprised the character in the 2019 sequel, Glass, completing a nearly two-decade-long trilogy.

Jackson’s depiction of Nick Fury, the enigmatic head of S.H.I.E.L.D. in the Marvel Cinematic Universe, was another watershed moment in his career. He initially appeared as Fury in a post-credits sequence in Iron Man, and he has since reprised the character in other Marvel films, including The Avengers and Captain America: The Winter Soldier.

Aside from his blockbuster parts, Jackson has regularly played a wide range of characters, demonstrating his flexibility as an actor. He has proved his ability to command the screen and offer depth to every role he portrays —  from his chilling performance as the diabolical Richmond Valentine in Kingsman: The Secret Service to his depiction of the famed abolitionist in The Hateful Eight.

Aside from his acting abilities, Jackson’s services to the film business have been recognized through several awards. He has garnered a BAFTA, a Golden Globe, and three NAACP Image Awards. 

Jackson’s career displays his enormous brilliance, flexibility, and devotion to his profession. He has continuously provided remarkable performances that have amused and inspired audiences, from his early stage appearances to his famous parts in blockbusters. 

He has made an everlasting effect on the entertainment business and will be remembered as one of the best performers of his time, thanks to his commanding presence, captivating charisma, and undeniable skill.

Upcoming Ridley Scott Movies — Insight on His New and Next Releases

Ridley Scott has a slate of upcoming movies and series in the pipeline.

English filmmaker Ridley Scott has garnered widespread acclaim for his directorial and producer contributions, particularly in the realms of science fiction, crime, and historical drama genres.

Scott kicked off his filmmaking journey with The Duellists in 1977. Since then, he has directed a wide range of movies, including The Martian, Alien: Covenant, Kingdom of Heaven, and many more.

He has also served as a producer for the first season of Amazon’s The Man in the High Castle, the dark comic science-fiction series BrainDead, the BBC/FX three-part miniseries A Christmas Carol, and numerous other projects.

Over his illustrious three-decade career, the filmmaker has received three Academy Award nominations for Directing, and he is recognized for his outstanding work on Thelma & Louise, Gladiator, and Black Hawk Down.

Adding to his impressive list of achievements, he secured two wins: one for Outstanding Television Film with the HBO production The Gathering Storm and another for Outstanding Documentary or Nonfiction Special with the History Channel’s Gettysburg.

Recently, Scott directed the film Napoleon, which has crossed the $200 million mark globally. His new movie tells the captivating story of the rise and fall of French Emperor Napoleon Bonaparte, focusing on his intense relationship with his wife, Josephine.

He has also produced the mystery film A Haunting in Venice, which hit theaters in the United States on September 15, 2023, under 20th Century Studios. The movie received positive reviews from critics and has grossed $122 million worldwide.

Additionally, the filmmaker has a lineup of upcoming movies in both producer and director roles. Some of the projects he is producing include Berlin Nobody, Alien: Romulus, Emma’s War, First Ascent, Panopticon, Blade Runner 2099, Echo Valley, David, The Cartel, War Party, and so on.

Similarly, as a director, he has several upcoming movies and TV shows, including Sinking Spring, Gladiator 2, Roads to Freedom, Queen & Country, and Wraiths of the Broken Land. More details about these upcoming Ridley Scott movies and TV shows are below!

‘Gladiator 2’

Gladiator 2 is an upcoming epic historical drama that serves as a sequel to the acclaimed Gladiator (2000). It was produced by Scott Free Productions for Universal Pictures and Paramount Pictures.

The drama unfolds by following Lucius, the son of Maximus’ beloved Lucilla, in the aftermath of Maximus’ death. As a sequel to the original masterpiece, Gladiator 2 holds promise in exploring new dimensions within the historical epic genre.

Some of the cast members in Gladiator 2 include Paul Mescal as Lucius Verus, Connie Nielsen as the mother of Lucius, Derek Jacobi as Senator Gracchus, and Joseph Quinn as co-emperor Caracalla, among others.

As per IMDb, Roads to Freedom is a forthcoming 10-episode epic series set to narrate the intricate story of World War II from diverse international perspectives.

‘Queen & Country’

Queen & Country follows the story of a Special Ops agent who is on the run after assassinating a powerful figure in Eastern Europe. The screenplay is crafted by Olivia Milch and Greg Rucka, with Sylvia Hoeks leading the cast.

The movie is still in the development phase of production, and details such as the release date are yet to be revealed, creating an element of mystery and anticipation for the upcoming film.

Top 5 Ridley Scott Movies on Netflix to Watch This Weekend

Get your popcorns ready!

Sir Ridley Scott is a British film director. He is most recognized for his work as a director of science fiction, crime, and historical drama films, like the Alien film series.

His work is characterized by an ambient and highly focused visual approach. Scott has won several awards, including the BAFTA Fellowship for Lifetime Achievement in 2018, two Primetime Emmys, and a Golden Globe. 

He has even been knighted by the late Queen Elizabeth II in 2003. Scott, a graduate of the Royal College of Art in London, began his career in television as a designer and director before going into advertising as a commercial director. 

He made his directorial debut with The Duellists in 1977 and rose to prominence with his following feature, Alien, in 1979. The Alien film was shown in 70 mm at midnight on May 25, 1979, as the opening night of the fourth Seattle International Film Festival. 

It received negative reviews upon its first release but went on to become a box-office triumph, earning the Academy Award for Best Visual Effects, three Saturn Awards —  Best Science Fiction Film, Best Direction for Scott, and Best Supporting Actress for Cartwright —  and a Hugo Award for Best Dramatic Presentation.

Alien’s popularity produced a media empire of films, novels, video games, and toys. The story of Sigourney Weaver’s character’s interactions with the alien species served as the conceptual and narrative foundation for the sequels: Aliens (1986), Alien 3 (1992), and Alien Resurrection (1997). 

The Alien vs. Predator films were created through a collaboration with the Predator brand, and Scott helmed a two-film prequel series. While the Alien film franchise is not on Netflix, there are various other movies by Scott on the streaming platform. 

Here are the top five Ridley Scott movies on Netflix. Note: The availability of these movies on Netflix is based on where you live.

Blade Runner: The Final Cut (1982)

The 1982 science fiction film Blade Runner was directed by Scott and written by Hampton Fancher and David Peoples. It is an adaptation of Philip K. Dick’s 1968 novel Do Androids Dream of Electric Sheep, starring Harrison Ford, Rutger Hauer, Sean Young, and Edward James Olmos. 

The film is set in a dystopian future Los Angeles of 2019 when the powerful Tyrell Corporation has bio-engineered synthetic people known as replicants to work on space colonies. When a crew of sophisticated replicants commanded by Roy Batty (Hauer) flees to Earth, burnt-out detective Rick Deckard (Ford) grudgingly agrees to track them down.

As a consequence of controversial alterations sought by studio management, seven separate versions of Blade Runner exist. Following a positive reaction to test screenings of a workprint, a director’s cut was released in 1992. 

This, combined with the film’s success as a video rental, made it one of the first films to be released on DVD. The Final Cut, a 25th-anniversary digitally restored version, was released by Warner Bros. in 2007; this is the only version over which Scott kept artistic control.

Gladiator (2000)

Gladiator, directed by Scott and written by David Franzoni, John Logan, and William Nicholson, is a 2000 epic historical drama film. DreamWorks Pictures distributed it in North America, while Universal Pictures distributed it overseas through United International Pictures. 

Russell Crowe, Joaquin Phoenix, Connie Nielsen, Tomas Arana, Ralf Möller, Oliver Reed, Djimon Hounsou, Derek Jacobi, John Shrapnel, Richard Harris, and Tommy Flanagan are among the cast members.

Crowe plays Roman commander Maximus Decimus Meridius, who is deceived when Commodus, Emperor Marcus Aurelius’ ambitious son, murders his father and seizes the throne. 

Maximus is sold into slavery and becomes a gladiator, rising through the ranks of the arena to avenge the killings of his family and the emperor. Gladiator has been critically reevaluated since its first release. 

It is now largely regarded as one of the best films of the 2000s, as well as one of the best historical epics ever filmed. Scott recently stated that work on the sequel, which is set to be published in the United States on November 22, 2024, has begun.

The Counselor (2013)

The Counsellor is a 2013 criminal thriller film directed by Scott and written by Cormac McCarthy. It stars Penélope Cruz, Cameron Diaz, Javier Bardem, and Brad Pitt alongside Michael Fassbender as the titular Counsellor. 

In the background of the Mexican drug trade, the film explores issues such as greed, mortality, love, and trust. As the Counsellor, a high-level lawyer, becomes entangled in a cocaine trade near the volatile Ciudad Juarez, Mexico/Texas border area, the extraordinarily violent and ruthless actions of drug gangs are revealed.

The Counsellor was the closing picture of the 2013 Morelia Film Festival, and it also appeared at the Cork Film Festival. The film was released in theatres on October 25, 2013, and is dedicated to Scott’s late brother, Tony.

It garnered mixed reviews and grossed $71 million worldwide on a $25 million budget.

The Martian (2015)

The Martian, directed by Scott and starring Matt Damon, is a 2015 science fiction film. Drew Goddard developed the script from Andy Weir’s 2011 novel of the same name. 

The movie recounts an astronaut’s lone fight to live on Mars after being abandoned, as well as NASA’s efforts to rescue him and return him to Earth. Jessica Chastain, Jeff Daniels, Kristen Wiig, Chiwetel Ejiofor, Sean Bean, Michael Pea, Kate Mara, Sebastian Stan, Aksel Hennie, Mackenzie Davis, Donald Glover, and Benedict Wong all appear in the film.

It garnered favorable reviews and earned more than $630 million worldwide, making it Scott’s highest-grossing picture to date and the tenth-highest-grossing film of 2015. The film gained critical acclaim for its directing, visual effects, musical soundtrack, narrative, scientific correctness, and likability. 

It was nominated for seven Academy Awards, and it garnered countless additional honors.

All the Money in the World (2017)

Ridley Scott’s 2017 biographical criminal thriller film All the Money in the World is directed by Scott and written by David Scarpa.

Based on John Pearson’s 1995 book Painfully Rich: The Outrageous Fortunes and Misfortunes of J. Paul Getty’s Heirs, it depicts the events surrounding the kidnapping of John Paul Getty III in 1973 and his grandfather’s refusal to cooperate with the kidnappers’ extortion demands. 

The title of the movie comes from an early conversation between Gail Harris (Getty III’s mother) and Dante ‘Cinquanta’ Agnana (one of Getty III’s abductors). When Harris states she can’t pay the ransom because she doesn’t have any money, Aganana responds angrily, “Get it from your father-in-law. He has all the money in the world.”

Michelle Williams plays Gail Harris Getty, John Paul Getty III’s mother, Christopher Plummer plays Getty, and Mark Wahlberg plays Fletcher Chase, a Getty family adviser.

On December 18, 2017, the film debuted at the Samuel Goldwyn Theatre in Beverly Hills, followed by a theatrical release in the United States by TriStar Pictures on December 25, 2017. It garnered mostly positive reviews, with many praising the performances, and earned $57 million against a $50 million budget. 

Plummer garnered special praise for his performance, and he was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor for his role in the picture.
