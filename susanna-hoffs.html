Attended Palisades High School

Susanna Hoffs was born on January 17, 1959, in Los Angeles, California, to a Jewish family. Tamar Ruth and Joshua Allen Hoffs are her parents. The Bangs’ co-founder

Susanna Hoffs and her friends Debbi Peterson and Vicki Peterson formed the punk band The Bangs. Hoffs was heavily influenced by the Ramones and other punk bands. The Bangs’ debut album, The Night Before It Was Passed, was released in 2008. Following a legal lawsuit by an East Coast boy band, they renamed their band The Bangles.

Career as a member of ‘The Bangles’

The Bangles released their debut full-length album, All Over the Place, on Columbia Records in 1984. Different Light, released in 1986, was one of the band’s early mainstream breakthrough albums. It contained chart-topping hits such as Manic Monday, If She Knew What She Wanted, and Walk Like an Egyptian.

In 1988, The Bangles released their third studio album, Everything. Hoffs wrote the song ‘In Your Room,’ which was published by the band the same year. The track finally charted in the top ten. Unfortunately, The Bangles split up in 1989 due to a misunderstanding, but they reformed a year later. The band then released another hit, Get the Girl. In addition, the band released their fourth album, Doll Revolution, in 2003.

Career as an Actor

Susanna Hoffs made her acting debut in Andrew Davis’s 1978 film Stony Island. Her other work include The Haircut and The Allnighter. Hoffs co-starred in Austin Powers: International Man of Mystery in 1997, opposite Elizabeth Hurley and Mimi Rogers. She re-appeared in the sequels The Spy Who Shagged Me and Austin Powers in Goldmember. In 2012, Hoffs participated in the television series Comedy Bang! Bang!
