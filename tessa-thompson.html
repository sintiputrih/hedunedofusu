Tessa Lynne Thompson is an American actress and musician best known for her roles on Veronica Mars as Jackie Cook and in the film Creed as Bianca. Where was Tessa Thompson born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Tessa Thompson, the star of Veronica Mars, was born in Los Angeles, California, on October 3, 1983. Tessa is an American citizen with a mixed ethnic background; her father, singer-songwriter Marc Anthony Thompson, is of Panamanian descent, and her mother is half-Mexican and half-European. Thompson was born in Los Angeles as well as Brooklyn, New York City. She attended Santa Monica High School and was involved in many of its theater productions. She majored in cultural anthropology at Santa Monica College after graduating from high school.

How did Tessa Thompson start her Professional Career?

Tessa Thompson was discovered in Hollywood while walking with her father as a high school student. Following that, in 2002, she made her professional stage debut in Los Angeles with the Women’s Shakespeare Company’s production of The Tempest.

Thompson was nominated for an NAACP Theater Award for her role as Juliet in Romeo and Juliet: Antebellum New Orleans after a year. She also appeared alongside Mahershala Ali and Joshua Jackson in Lydia R. Diamond’s play Smart People at Second Stage Theater.

Thompson also appeared in Cold Case, a CBS sitcom that aired in 2005. In 2006, she appeared in the Tessa Thompson medical drama Grey’s Anatomy.

Film and music

Tessa Thompson made her film debut as Scarlet in the 2006 remake of the horror film When a Stranger Calls. Despite a production cost of only $15 million, the film had earned a total of $47.9 million at the domestic box office and a total of $67 million worldwide as of March 19, 2006.

In 2015, Tessa appeared in Nate Ruess’ short film The Grand Romantic. As a member of the electro band Caught A Ghost, she wrote and produced music that was featured in the film Dear White People.
