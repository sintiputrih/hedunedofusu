Why Is Lamelo Ball Getting Sued?

Publish date: 2024-06-07

Charlotte Hornets guard, LaMelo Ball is on the wrong side of a legal issue. He has been named in a lawsuit filed against him and the Hornets, seeking $25,000 in damages in an incident stemming from Hornets Purple and Teal day before the season began. The lawsuit alleges Ball "suddenly and without warning accelerated his vehicle forward" at a traffic light, hitting an 11-year-old fan who was trying to get his autograph. The suit alleges that the child endured extreme foot and back injuries and pain.
It should be mentioned that this is not the first time Ball has been in legal trouble. A year later in 2022, the former publicist sued him, claiming he had refused to pay her money she was due after securing him high-paying endorsement deals. He was included in a lawsuit against his family in late 2023 by a co-founder of the Big Baller Brand for doing effectively the same thing to him, stealing him away from the business. This is trouble awaiting - his off-court behavior opens itself up as a question in legal entanglements, particularly as the franchise turns the page to a new era with a new head coach, Charles Lee.
The lawsuit also called attention to Ball's driving habits, including images of him leaving the Hornets facility at a high speed emerging on social media as well. The incident happened following an annual preseason fan fest at the Hornets' arena in uptown Charlotte, North Carolina. According to WFLA, Ball is accused of running his SUV over the foot of an 11-year-old fan as the boy tried to approach Ball's vehicle for an autograph, causing severe physical and emotional trauma.
