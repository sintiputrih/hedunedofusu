WWE WrestleMania 39 Breaks Last Years Ticket Sales Record

Publish date: 2024-04-17

WrestleMania 39 is one of the most highly anticipated Premium Live Events in recent memory. Being such a huge attraction, it comes as no surprise that fans will try to get in on the action and buy tickets. In fact, it seems the show has surpassed the ticket sales of last year’s WrestleMania already.

SoFi Stadium in Inglewood, California will host an event which will see John Cena and Austin Theory will compete for the United States Championship. Cody Rhodes will also challenge Roman Reigns for the Undisputed WWE Universal Championship. Due to such high profile matches, it’s inevitable that fans will watch to watch the event.

According to WrestleTix, in 2022, over 130,166 tickets were sold out combined on both nights. However, WrestleMania 39 has sold 130,807 so far overall for both nights.

For Night One, 65,479 seats have sold out of a total capacity of 66,302, leaving only 823 tickets available to buy. However, the company is adding new seats for the first night at the event.

For Night Two, 66,328 tickets have been purchased out of of a 66,509 overall capacity. Only 181 seats are available now, so the event is more or less a sellout at this point in time.

WrestleMania 39 is a highly anticipated event and the ticket sales speak for themselves right now. We will have to wait and see just how memorable the two-night event will turn out this weekend on April 1st and 2nd.
